Understanding Blue Carbon

Blue carbon refers to carbon captured and stored by coastal and marine ecosystems - primarily mangroves, seagrasses, and salt marshes.

These ecosystems sequester carbon at rates up to 10 times higher than terrestrial forests, making them critical allies in climate change mitigation.

  • check_circle 50% of carbon in marine sediments is captured by blue carbon ecosystems
  • check_circle Mangroves can store up to 10 times more carbon than terrestrial forests
  • check_circle Blue carbon projects provide crucial biodiversity and coastal protection benefits
